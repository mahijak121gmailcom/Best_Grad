Top Universities In Abroad

UK Universities

University of west of Scotland

June 26, 2015

The University of the West of Scotland, formerly the University of Paisley, is a public university with four campuses in south-western Scotland, in the towns of Paisley, Blantyre, Dumfries and Ayr, as well as a campus in London, England.

University of west of Scotland

University of Roehampton

August 22, 2015

The University of Roehampton, formerly Roehampton Institute of Higher Education, is a public university in the United Kingdom, situated on three major sites in Roehampton, in the London Borough of Wandsworth.

University of Roehampton

Edinburgh Napier University

January 14, 2016

Edinburgh Napier University is a public university in Edinburgh, Scotland. Napier Technical College, the predecessor of the university, was founded in 1964, taking its name from Scottish mathematician and philosopher John Napier.

Edinburgh Napier University

Falmouth University

December 23, 2016

Falmouth University is a specialist University for the creative industries based in Falmouth and Penryn, Cornwall, England. Founded as the Falmouth School of Art in 1902, it has previously been known as Falmouth College of Art and Design.

Falmouth University

Bournemouth University

September 13, 2015

Bournemouth University is a public university in Bournemouth, Dorset, England, with its main campus situated in neighbouring Poole. The university was founded in 1992; however, the origins of its predecessor date back to the early 1900s.

Bournemouth University

University of Plymouth

June 12, 2014

The University of Plymouth is a public university based predominantly in Plymouth, England where the main campus is located. The university has campuses and affiliated colleges across South West England. With 19,645 students, its is the 38th largest university in the UK.

University of Plymouth
USA Universities

Marshall University

March 18, 2015

Marshall University is a public research university in Huntington, West Virginia. It was founded in 1837 and is named after John Marshall, the fourth Chief Justice of the United States. The university is currently composed of nine undergraduate colleges.

Marshall University

University of South Florida

August 22, 2014

The University of South Florida is a public research university in Tampa, Florida. It is a member institution of the State University System of Florida. Founded in 1956, USF is the fourth-largest public university in the state of Florida, with an enrollment of 50,927 in 2019–2020.

University of South Florida

The University of Alabama at Birmingham

May 14, 2016

The University of Alabama at Birmingham is a public research university. Developed from an academic extension center established in 1936, the institution became a four-year campus in 1966 and a fully autonomous university in the University of Alabama System.

The University of Alabama at Birmingham

Saint Louis University

July 23, 2016

Saint Louis University is a private Jesuit research university with campuses in St. Louis, Missouri, United States, and Madrid, Spain. Founded in 1818 by Louis Guillaume Valentin Dubourg, it is the oldest university west of the Mississippi River and the second-oldest Jesuit university in the United States.

Saint Louis University
Canadian Institutions

George Brown College

October 27, 2014

George Brown College of Applied Arts and Technology is a public, fully accredited college of applied arts and technology with three full campuses in downtown Toronto. Like many other colleges in Ontario, GBC was chartered in 1966 by the government of Ontario and opened the next year.

George Brown College

Lambton College

June 12, 2016

Lambton College is a publicly funded college in Sarnia, Ontario, Canada. It has approximately 3,500 full-time students, 6,500 part-time students and 3,500 international students worldwide. Lambton College also has campuses in Mississauga and Toronto.

Lambton College

Seneca College

August 14, 2016

Seneca College of Applied Arts and Technology is a multiple-campus public college located in the Greater Toronto Area of Ontario, Canada. It offers full-time and part-time programs at the baccalaureate, diploma, certificate and graduate levels.

Seneca College

Canadore College

July 21, 2015

Canadore College is a college of applied arts and technology located in North Bay, Ontario, Canada. It was founded in 1967 as a campus of Sudbury's Cambrian College, and became an independent institution in 1972. Canadore College has four campuses in North Bay, Ontario, and a campus in Parry Sound, Ontario.

Canadore College
